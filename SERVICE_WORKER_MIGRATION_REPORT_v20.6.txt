MAURITIUS MASS FINDER v20.6 — SERVICE-WORKER MIGRATION REPORT

Problem:
- Existing installed users on v17.2 were controlled by sw.js.
- v20.5 registered service-worker.js.
- The installed v17.2 app could fetch fresh version.json and detect v20.5/v20.6, but its refresh action could still reload old cached index.html/app.js.

Fix:
1. Added root-level sw.js back into the release package.
2. Converted sw.js into a migration bridge, not the primary long-term app worker.
3. sw.js now:
   - calls skipWaiting on install;
   - deletes existing caches on activate;
   - claims clients;
   - navigates open windows with a cache-busting query;
   - serves same-origin GET requests network-first with no-store.
4. Updated service-worker.js to v20.6 and made critical shell files network-first.
5. Updated app.js refresh action to clear stale app caches more aggressively before reload.

Why sw.js must remain in v20.6:
- Users already installed on v17.2 will only update their existing service-worker registration by fetching sw.js.
- Removing sw.js prevents that legacy registration from receiving the migration script.

Deployment instruction:
- Upload both sw.js and service-worker.js.
- Do not delete sw.js until a future version after legacy users have had time to migrate.

Recommended live test:
- Open the currently installed v17.2 PWA.
- Wait for the update banner.
- Tap Refresh / Update.
- Confirm footer/version changes to 20.6.
- Confirm the app no longer loops on “new version available.”
